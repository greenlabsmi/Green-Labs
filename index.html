Learn Before You Buy

Quick answers. Better purchases. Less regret.

Explore common cannabis questions, then use our quick quiz to find the vibe that fits you best.

What are terpenes?

Terpenes are the natural aromatic compounds in cannabis that influence smell, flavor, and how a strain tends to feel.

Why do terpenes matter more than THC %?

THC tells you how potent something is, but terpenes help shape whether the experience feels uplifting, relaxing, clear-headed, or heavy.

What are common cannabis terpenes?

Myrcene = relaxing, body-heavy

Limonene = bright, upbeat, mood-friendly

Pinene = clear, fresh, focused

Caryophyllene = grounding, spicy, calming

Linalool = floral, soft, relaxing

How should I shop by terpene?

Look for products with terpene info on the label, or ask your budtender what strains are dominant in the effects you want.

What does THC do?

THC is the main cannabinoid responsible for the psychoactive “high.”

What does CBD do?

CBD is generally more calming and non-intoxicating, and many people use it for a more balanced experience.

What is a beginner edible dose?

Beginner: 2.5–5mg THC

Moderate: 5–10mg THC

Experienced: 10mg+ THC

How long do edibles take?

Most edibles take 30–90 minutes to start and can last several hours. Start low and wait before taking more.

Does indica always mean sleepy?

Not always. Those labels are broad and often oversimplified. Effects depend more on the terpene profile, cannabinoids, and your body.

Does sativa always mean energetic?

No. Some “sativas” can still feel heavy, and some hybrids can feel very uplifting.

Why are these labels less useful now?

Most modern cannabis is hybridized, so the old labels are less predictive than terpene and cannabinoid data.

What should I use instead?

Shop by effect goal: relax, focus, mood, sleep, body relief, or social vibe.

What hits fastest?

Flower and vapes hit much faster than edibles.

What lasts the longest?

Edibles usually last the longest and can feel stronger for many people.

What’s best for beginners?

Low-dose edibles, balanced gummies, or a carefully guided flower recommendation can all work — it depends on comfort level.

What’s the most traditional experience?

Flower gives the fullest terpene-driven experience and is the classic go-to.

What should I tell my budtender?

Say how you want to feel, your tolerance, and what format you like most.

What should I ignore?

Don’t rely only on strain names or THC percentage.

What should I look for instead?

Ask about terpene profile, freshness, genetics, and the actual experience people report.

What makes Dutch Deli different?

Our deli-style setup helps you explore flower more intentionally, with guidance based on aroma, terpene profile, and the experience you actually want.
